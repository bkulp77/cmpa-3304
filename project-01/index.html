About Me

My name is Brian Kulp, as a combat systems inspector for the United States Department of the Navy on the Board of Inspection and Survey, I leverage my extensive experience and expertise in radio and communications systems to conduct inspections and testing, document deficiencies, and prepare detailed reports for senior military leadership and Congress. I have a proven track record of utilizing expert knowledge in UHF/VHF radio systems, including Motorola handhelds, for accurate assessments.

In addition to my current role, I have held various positions in the defense industry, including Product Support Manager at L3Harris Technologies and Maintenance Manager/Project Manager/2M Gold Disk Developer at Assault Craft Unit Four and many other duty stations over 20 years in the United States Navy. In these roles, I developed and maintained user's logistics support summary and system maintenance manual, managed COTS configuration management, and directed daily operations of 24 electronics maintenance personnel. I hold multiple degrees and certifications in engineering and technology management, project management, business administration, and lean six sigma black belt. I am passionate about applying my skills and knowledge to ensure the highest standards of quality and efficiency in the defense sector.

Projects

  1. CIAT Technical Manual

    Authored and maintained the User’s Logistics Support Summary, Maintenance Manual, and training documentation for the Combined Integrated Air and Missile Defense and Anti-Submarine Warfare Trainer (CIAT).

  2. CIAT Configuration Management Website

    Developed and maintained the component database in Configuration Status Accounting (CSA) and Integrated Product Data Management (iPDM) to include product support documentation, hardware/software configurations, trouble ticket forms and database with the help of a software engineer.

  3. Carrier Strike Group Key Performance Indicators

    Created and presented key performance indicators for the Chief of Staff and Admiral of the strike group that tracked equipment casualties to identify trends in equipment readiness and maintenance downtime, as well as lead times for supply procurement of repair parts. Metrics covered 18 months of data and over 3,100 casualty reports, equipment classification to determine if departments within the group had any abnormal reporting to identify issues with procedural compliance. Regression analysis used to determine if there was a change in maintenance or reporting trends.

  4. Shipboard LAN Upgrade

    Managed a support team responsible for configuring the staff building for move off of USS Nimitz, the team invested over 900 man-hours installed 55 workstations to include the installation of over 100 NMCI computers, 45 telephones, and a new video teleconference suite. Created drawings for CANES LAN upgrade for USS Nimitz to show the requirements for the staff’s computing and video display boards, which consisted of over 200 drops. This saved the Navy thousands of dollars in rework and having to change the scope of the job later by properly identifying the requirements in advance.
